## I-35: The Heartland's Commercial Spine

I-35 stretches 1,568 miles from Laredo, Texas, on the Mexican border to Duluth, Minnesota, at the Canadian border zone. It is the primary north-south freight artery for the central United States, funneling Mexican cross-border freight northward through Texas and connecting the manufacturing and agricultural heartland through Oklahoma, Kansas, Missouri, Iowa, and Minnesota.

The corridor splits in the Kansas City/Wichita zone: I-35 branches into I-35E through Dallas and I-35W through Fort Worth in Texas, then rejoins north of the DFW Metroplex. Both branches are heavily traveled by commercial trucks. The corridor's character shifts significantly by region: South Texas and northern Mexico produce cross-border freight dominated by manufactured goods and agricultural products. The Oklahoma City hub is a major freight distribution point. Kansas City is one of the busiest intermodal hubs in the country. Iowa is deep agricultural country, and the Minnesota terminal at the Twin Cities connects to the Upper Midwest rail and distribution network.

Understanding which stops work on I-35 — and where the fuel price opportunities exist — is essential for drivers who run this corridor regularly.

## Texas: Laredo to the Red River

The Texas segment of I-35 runs from the Mexican border at Laredo through San Antonio, Austin, and the Dallas-Fort Worth Metroplex, then north to the Oklahoma border.

**Love's Travel Stop at Laredo, TX (I-35 exit 2):** The standard pre-crossing staging stop for northbound traffic from Mexico. The Laredo Love's is a high-volume facility with solid amenities geared toward cross-border freight operations. Fuel pricing here is competitive — Texas has low fuel taxes, and Laredo's Love's competes with several independent fueling options in the border zone.

**Pilot Flying J at San Antonio, TX (I-35 at Probandt Street):** One of several Pilot and Flying J locations in the San Antonio metro area. San Antonio is a practical rest stop for northbound drivers who have cleared the Laredo-to-SA run in one push. The Loop 1604 area on I-35 has multiple options; the Pilot at Probandt is centrally located for drivers staying on I-35 rather than routing through downtown.

**Love's Travel Stop at New Braunfels, TX (I-35 exit 186):** New Braunfels sits between San Antonio and Austin and is a practical fuel stop for drivers breaking the SA-Austin segment. Good parking, Speedco on site, competitive Texas fuel pricing.

**Pilot Flying J at Waco, TX (I-35 exit 335):** Waco is the midpoint stop between Austin and the DFW Metroplex. The Waco Pilot is a large, well-maintained facility — around 200 truck parking spaces — and is a common overnight staging point for drivers who want to hit DFW at off-peak hours. Running DFW between 9:00 PM and 6:00 AM avoids the worst of the metro congestion.

**Pilot Flying J at Hillsboro, TX (I-35 exit 368):** Where I-35E and I-35W rejoin north of the Metroplex. Hillsboro is a busy interchange stop with good amenities and serves as both a fuel stop and a staging point for drivers routing to either the Dallas or Fort Worth branches.

## Oklahoma: Red River to Kansas

Oklahoma is Love's home territory. The chain's headquarters is in Oklahoma City, and their network density in the state is unmatched.

**Love's Travel Stop at Denton, TX/Gainesville, TX (I-35 exits near state line):** The Love's at Gainesville is the last Texas stop before the Oklahoma border — useful for topping off Texas diesel before entering Oklahoma's slightly different pricing environment.

**Love's Travel Stop at Ardmore, OK (I-35 exit 31):** A large, well-equipped Love's in southern Oklahoma. Good parking, clean facilities, and competitive fuel pricing. Ardmore is also the site of a Love's truck tire center, making it a practical stop if you are managing tire wear on a long run.

**Pilot Flying J at Oklahoma City, OK (I-35 at SE 59th Street):** Oklahoma City is a major freight hub and the Love's/Pilot corridor competition is at its most intense here. Multiple large stops cluster around the I-35/I-40 interchange — one of the most competitive fuel pricing clusters in the south-central US. If you are flexible on brand, compare app pricing at the cluster before committing.

**Love's Travel Stop at Edmond, OK (I-35 exit 141):** North of OKC, the Edmond Love's is a modern facility well-positioned for northbound drivers who cleared the city and want to fuel before the Kansas run. Good parking availability compared to the more congested OKC stops.

**Flying J at Guthrie, OK (I-35 exit 157):** Another solid option north of OKC for fuel and a short break before the Kansas border.

## Kansas: The Open Plains Run

Kansas on I-35 runs through Wichita, which splits I-35 from the eastern Kansas Turnpike (Kansas Turnpike, K-TA) connector toward I-70. The I-35 mainline continues northeast toward Kansas City.

**Love's Travel Stop at Purcell, OK / Emporia, KS area:** Multiple Love's and Pilot locations anchor the southern Kansas approach. Emporia, KS, sits at the junction of I-335 (Kansas Turnpike) and I-35, making it a hub for both routes.

**Pilot Flying J at Wichita, KS (I-35 / I-235 interchange area):** Wichita is the primary metro stop on the Kansas segment. Multiple Pilot and Love's locations serve the city — the stops near the I-135/I-235 interchange on the north side are the most practical for through-traffic drivers who do not need to make city deliveries.

**TA Truckstop at Emporia, KS (I-35 exit 127):** Emporia's TA is a full-service stop at the I-335/I-35 junction, well-positioned for drivers routing to or from Topeka and I-70. Good repair facilities and reliable parking make it a standout on the Kansas segment.

**Love's Travel Stop at Lenexa, KS (I-35 exit 224):** The last major Kansas stop before Kansas City. Lenexa's Love's is modern and well-maintained, though parking tightens during evening peak as drivers stage for the KC metro crossing.

## Missouri: Kansas City to the Iowa Border

I-35 runs northeast through Kansas City and continues to the Iowa border at a relatively modest distance — the Missouri segment is comparatively short.

**Pilot Flying J at North Kansas City, MO (I-35 exit 6):** The anchor stop north of the Kansas City metro. The North KC Pilot is a large facility serving both I-35 northbound traffic and I-70 corridor drivers. Good amenities, solid parking, and the fuel pricing benefits of Kansas City competition.

**Love's Travel Stop at Cameron, MO (I-35 exit 54):** North of KC, Cameron is the standard next stop before entering Iowa. Clean facilities, good parking availability, and competitive pricing. The Cameron Love's is a practical overnight staging point for drivers who cleared KC and want to make Iowa in the morning.

## Iowa: Ag Country Hauling

Iowa's I-35 segment runs from the Missouri border through Des Moines and north to the Minnesota line. Agricultural freight, particularly in harvest season, means elevated commercial traffic and tighter parking availability.

**Pilot Flying J at Osceola, IA (I-35 exit 12):** The first major Iowa stop after crossing from Missouri. The Osceola Pilot is a solid full-service facility with good parking and is well-used by agricultural freight drivers on the corridor. Fuel pricing in Iowa is generally competitive.

**Love's Travel Stop at Des Moines, IA (multiple I-35 exits):** Des Moines is a significant freight hub with multiple stops on I-35. The Love's at Ankeny (I-35 exit 92) and the Pilot at Altoona are both well-maintained and within reasonable proximity for drivers needing to fuel or rest in the capital area.

**Pilot Flying J at Ames, IA (I-35 exit 113):** North of Des Moines, the Ames Pilot serves both I-35 and US-30 traffic. Good amenities and reliable parking.

**Love's Travel Stop at Clear Lake, IA (I-35 exit 194):** The final major Iowa stop before the Minnesota border. Clear Lake's Love's is a practical overnight staging point for northbound drivers targeting the Twin Cities in the morning.

## Minnesota: Twin Cities to Duluth

**Pilot Flying J at Owatonna, MN (I-35 exit 41):** The first full-service stop in Minnesota on I-35, approximately 60 miles south of the Twin Cities. Owatonna's Pilot is a large facility with good amenities — a reliable stop for drivers staging before the metro approach.

**TA Truckstop at Burnsville, MN (I-35W exit 2):** Near the southern metro periphery, Burnsville's TA is one of the better-equipped stops in the Twin Cities market. Full restaurant, showers, repair shop, and adequate parking for a metro-adjacent facility.

**Love's Travel Stop at Wyoming, MN (I-35 exit 147):** North of the Twin Cities on I-35 heading toward Duluth, the Wyoming Love's is a solid stop for northbound drivers who have cleared the metro.

## I-35 Fuel Strategy

**Oklahoma is your best fueling state.** Love's dense network, competitive pricing driven by their home-state concentration, and low Oklahoma state fuel taxes make the Oklahoma segment the optimal tankering zone for I-35 drivers. Fill up in Oklahoma City or Edmond before heading north.

**Avoid urban premium at Dallas-Fort Worth.** Fuel prices inside the DFW Metroplex run above the Texas state average due to operating costs. Fuel at Hillsboro, Waco, or New Braunfels instead of metro-area stops.

**Plan Des Moines arrivals.** The Des Moines metro is a parking pinch point during harvest season. If you are running I-35 in October or November, add buffer time to your evening stop planning around Des Moines.
