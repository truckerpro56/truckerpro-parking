## Why Fuel Is the Most Controllable Variable Cost in Trucking

A Class 8 truck running 120,000 miles per year at 6.5 miles per gallon will consume approximately 18,500 gallons of diesel annually. At $4.00 per gallon, that is $74,000 in fuel. At $3.75 per gallon — a 25-cent-per-gallon savings — it drops to $69,375. The difference is $4,625 per year, or enough to cover a month of insurance premiums for an owner-operator.

Fuel price optimization is not glamorous work. It requires discipline in how you route, when you buy, where you buy, and how you drive. But it is consistently the single largest lever available to owner-operators and small carriers to improve their per-mile economics without changing their freight network. For large fleets running hundreds of trucks, even a half-cent-per-gallon improvement across the fleet translates to hundreds of thousands of dollars annually.

This guide covers every major fuel savings lever — price tracking, loyalty programs, fuel cards, route planning, and driving technique — in practical, actionable terms.

## Understanding Diesel Price Geography

Diesel prices vary significantly by state, province, and even by individual truck stop — sometimes by more than 30 cents per gallon between locations a few exits apart on the same highway. This variability is not random. It is driven by state and provincial fuel taxes, proximity to refineries, local competition, and the pricing strategies of individual truck stop chains.

**State Fuel Tax Variation (US)**

California imposes the highest total diesel tax in the US — over 90 cents per gallon when federal and state taxes are combined. By contrast, Wyoming, New Mexico, and Texas have some of the lowest combined tax rates, at well under 50 cents per gallon. For drivers running coast-to-coast, this means that filling up in California when you could have fueled in Arizona or Nevada is a structural overpayment.

The strategy implication is clear: carry enough fuel from low-tax states to get through high-tax states, or at minimum minimize the gallons you buy in high-cost jurisdictions. This is called "fuel tankering" and is standard practice among experienced long-haul drivers.

**Provincial Tax Variation (Canada)**

In Canada, provincial fuel taxes create similar disparities. Alberta has no provincial sales tax and lower fuel taxes than BC, Ontario, or Quebec. Drivers running the Trans-Canada should plan to fill up in Alberta before crossing into BC, and should prioritize filling in Ontario over Quebec when westbound.

**Price Differences at the Stop Level**

Even within a single state or province, individual truck stops vary their diesel rack pricing. The posted price at a Love's 10 miles off I-40 in Oklahoma may be 8 cents higher or lower than the Pilot 5 miles farther down the road. Price tracking apps and loyalty program pricing close some of this gap, but competitive monitoring pays.

## Price Tracking Tools

**GasBuddy / TruckPark**

GasBuddy's trucker-specific features include diesel prices at commercial fuel stops with user-reported pricing. Coverage is strong in the US; Canadian coverage is thinner. The data can lag reality by several hours during rapid price movement.

**Comdata CTS (Fuel Price Display)**

Comdata's platform for carriers includes real-time network pricing at participating locations. If your carrier uses Comdata, this is the most reliable source of actual negotiated pricing because it reflects what you would pay with your card — not rack pricing.

**EFS OneStop**

EFS's platform includes a fuel locator with network pricing for EFS cardholders. Similar to Comdata in utility — best for drivers with an EFS card.

**Pilot myRewards Plus App**

The Pilot myRewards Plus app shows diesel prices at Pilot and Flying J locations with real-time pricing updates. Importantly, it shows both the posted price and the price you will pay with myRewards enrollment — the difference is often 2–5 cents per gallon.

**Love's My Love Rewards App**

Love's app shows current diesel prices at Love's locations. It also shows your current reward status and shower credit balance, making it an efficient one-stop planning tool.

**TMS Fuel Analytics**

A properly configured TMS (Transportation Management System) tracks your actual cost-per-gallon, cost-per-mile, and fuel efficiency over time. This longitudinal data is more valuable than any single price comparison because it surfaces patterns: which corridors are consistently expensive, which stops in your regular lane tend to be priced above network average, and how your fuel efficiency varies by season, load type, or driver.

## Loyalty Programs: Building Your Fuel Savings Infrastructure

### Pilot Flying J myRewards Plus

myRewards Plus is Pilot Flying J's primary loyalty platform, replacing the older myPilot program. Key features:

- Earn points on every gallon purchased, redeemable for showers, food, merchandise, and in-cab Wi-Fi
- Discounts automatically applied at the pump when enrolled — typically 2–5 cents per gallon off posted price
- The myRewards Plus Mastercard earns additional points on all purchases, including non-fuel purchases
- Fleet tracking allows dispatcher visibility into driver fuel purchases and rewards balances
- Parking reservations integrated into the app at equipped locations

For owner-operators, myRewards Plus delivers the most value at higher fuel volumes. The more you buy at Pilot/Flying J locations, the better your pricing tier and the faster your reward accumulation.

### Love's My Love Rewards

My Love Rewards has a reputation among drivers as the most driver-friendly loyalty program in the industry, primarily because shower credits are earned quickly and expire slowly:

- Earn one shower credit for every 50 gallons of diesel (some promotions drop this to 30 gallons)
- Shower credits valid for 30 days, longer than most competitors
- Points redeemable for merchandise, food, and Speedco maintenance discounts
- My Love Rewards app consistently receives high ratings for reliability and usability

Love's also frequently runs promotional price-per-gallon discounts for My Love Rewards members on specific corridors, particularly in Oklahoma and Texas where they have the densest network.

### TA/Petro UltraONE

UltraONE is a tiered program: Silver, Gold, and Platinum levels unlock progressively better pricing. Achieving Platinum requires substantial annual volume but delivers:

- Best available per-gallon pricing in the TA/Petro network
- Free showers
- Priority access to Petro service bays
- Enhanced food discounts

For drivers who primarily run corridors where TA/Petro locations are concentrated — the Northeast, the Southeast, and I-81 in particular — UltraONE Platinum provides meaningful per-mile savings.

### Stacking Programs

Nothing prevents you from enrolling in all three programs and using whichever stop offers the best combination of price and amenities on a given day. Many experienced drivers maintain active enrollment in all three chains' programs and use GasBuddy or their TMS price data to decide which stop to use on any given corridor.

The practical limit on stacking is complexity: managing three apps, three card balances, and three sets of expiring rewards adds administrative overhead. For owner-operators, the simplest approach is to pick the chain with the strongest coverage on your primary lanes and maximize your volume there.

## Fuel Cards: The Fleet-Level Savings Layer

Fuel cards operate separately from loyalty programs. Where loyalty programs reward individual gallons, fuel cards deliver negotiated network discounts based on the card's network agreements. For owner-operators, a fuel card can deliver better per-gallon pricing than any retail loyalty program — but only if you route to the card's network stops.

**EFS (Electronic Funds Source)**

EFS is one of the largest commercial fuel card networks, accepted at approximately 5,000 locations in North America. EFS cards are widely used by carriers who issue them to owner-operators under lease agreements. Pricing is based on the carrier's network agreement, which is typically better than retail for drivers running high annual volumes.

**Comdata**

Comdata is the other dominant commercial fuel card platform, with extensive acceptance at Pilot Flying J, Love's, and many independent stops. Comdata also handles driver advances, settlements, and expense management — it is a comprehensive back-office tool for carriers.

**Relay Fuel Card**

Relay is a newer entrant focused on owner-operators and small fleets. It offers transparent pricing with a flat cents-per-gallon savings guarantee over posted retail, no minimum volume requirements, and a simple mobile-first experience. For independent owner-operators who do not have access to a carrier's negotiated Comdata or EFS rates, Relay is a strong option.

**OTR Fuel Card**

OTR markets aggressively to owner-operators with guaranteed savings of $0.25–$0.50 per gallon off posted retail at partner locations. Coverage is strong in the US; Canadian coverage is more limited.

**Choosing the Right Card**

The right fuel card depends on your operating pattern:

- If you run under a carrier and they offer you a Comdata or EFS card, use it — their negotiated rate almost certainly beats retail.
- If you are an independent owner-operator running predictable lanes, compare Relay or OTR against your current loyalty program pricing on those specific corridors.
- If you run into Canada regularly, ensure your US fuel card has Canadian acceptance — not all do.

## Route Planning for Fuel Savings

### Tankering Strategy

The most mechanically simple fuel savings strategy is to carry fuel from cheap jurisdictions through expensive ones. The constraint is your tank capacity and whether the total cost-per-mile of carrying extra weight (small but real) is outweighed by the price differential.

A standard rule: if the price differential between your current stop and the next jurisdiction exceeds 10 cents per gallon and you have tank capacity, fill up. For a 200-gallon fill, a 10-cent savings is $20. The weight penalty of 200 extra gallons of diesel (approximately 1,400 lbs) at typical freight rates is negligible.

### Routing Through Competitive Clusters

Truck stops near major interstate junctions — where multiple chains compete within a few miles — tend to have better pricing than isolated stops with no competition. The Love's/Pilot/TA cluster near the I-40/I-35 interchange in Oklahoma City is a classic example: competitive pricing driven by proximity.

Conversely, isolated stops in remote areas with no competition within 50 miles often price above network average. If you cannot avoid fueling at an isolated stop, minimize the gallons purchased and top up at a competitive cluster when you reach one.

### Avoiding Urban Premium Pricing

Diesel prices in major urban areas — Los Angeles, Chicago, New York, Seattle — are consistently above the state average due to operating costs, real estate, and environmental compliance costs. Route your fuel stops to suburban or exurban locations when possible.

## Driving Technique and Fuel Efficiency

No amount of fuel price optimization compensates for poor fuel efficiency. A 10% improvement in miles-per-gallon is worth far more than any loyalty program.

**Speed and Aerodynamic Drag**

Aerodynamic drag increases with the square of velocity. A truck running at 65 MPH uses approximately 15–20% more fuel than the same truck at 60 MPH. A truck running at 70 MPH uses roughly 25–30% more than at 60 MPH. The fuel-cost mathematics of speed are unambiguous: every MPH above 60 costs you money. On a 500-mile run, slowing from 65 to 60 saves approximately 2–3 gallons.

**Progressive Shifting and Engine RPM**

Modern automated transmissions optimize shift points for fuel efficiency, but if you are driving a manual or have control over shift points, keep the engine in the low-RPM power band. Operating at 1,250–1,450 RPM in the power band rather than 1,600–1,800 RPM in the torque band can improve fuel economy by 3–8%.

**Cruise Control on the Plains**

On flat terrain — Kansas, Oklahoma, Nebraska, the Great Plains generally — cruise control consistently outperforms manual throttle management for fuel economy. Human throttle response introduces micro-surges and throttle liftoffs that cruise control smooths out.

**Terrain-Aware Cruise (Predictive Cruise)**

Many modern tractors include predictive cruise control that reads upcoming terrain data and adjusts throttle ahead of grades. Volvo, Freightliner, and Kenworth all offer terrain-aware cruise as a factory or dealer option. If your truck has this feature and you are not using it, enable it — documented fuel savings of 2–4% are common.

**Pre-Trip HVAC and Idle Reduction**

Idling burns approximately 0.8 gallons per hour. A driver who idles an engine for 8 hours overnight instead of using a bunk heater burns 6.4 gallons unnecessarily. At $4.00/gallon, that is $25.60 per night or roughly $9,000 per year for a driver who sleeps every night in the cab.

APU (Auxiliary Power Unit) systems pay for themselves in 18–24 months for most owner-operators who frequently overnight in the cab. Idle reduction ordinances in California and many other states make APUs a compliance issue as well as a savings tool.

## Putting It Together: A Fuel Savings System

The drivers who consistently minimize fuel costs do not rely on any single tactic — they build a system:

1. **A primary loyalty program** matched to their most-traveled lanes
2. **A fuel card** that delivers negotiated pricing above the loyalty baseline
3. **A price tracking app** they check when planning tomorrow's fuel stops today
4. **A TMS** that tracks their actual cost-per-gallon over time and surfaces anomalies
5. **Speed discipline** — staying at or below 62 MPH on open highway
6. **Tankering habits** tuned to the price differentials on their regular lanes

None of these is individually transformative. Together, for an owner-operator running 120,000 miles annually, they can realistically represent $5,000–$10,000 per year in fuel savings compared to a driver doing none of them. That difference is significant against a typical owner-operator net income.

The fuel savings game is played over years, not miles. Start with the loyalty program and the price tracking app — the low-effort, high-return foundation — and build from there.
