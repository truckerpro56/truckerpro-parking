## What eManifest Is and Why It Matters

The Canada Border Services Agency (CBSA) requires commercial carriers crossing into Canada to file advance electronic cargo and conveyance information before arriving at the border. This system is called eManifest, and it operates under the Advance Commercial Information (ACI) program. The ACI program exists so that CBSA officers can assess cargo risk and make targeting decisions before a truck arrives at the primary inspection plaza — not while it is idling in the commercial lane.

For US drivers crossing into Canada, eManifest is not optional and it is not the broker's responsibility alone. The carrier and driver must ensure the filing is complete and accepted by CBSA before crossing. Filing errors, late submissions, or missing information can result in the carrier being directed to secondary inspection (adding 1–4 hours to a crossing), refused entry, or fined under CBSA enforcement.

The good news: once you understand the system, filing eManifest is a straightforward process. The challenge for US drivers is that the system is distinct from the US ACE eManifest used at the US border, the Canadian portal interface is different from CBP's tools, and the timing requirements are stricter than many US drivers initially expect.

## Who Is Responsible for What

ACI eManifest involves two separate filings that sometimes overlap in responsibility:

**Cargo data (shipment/freight):** The customs broker or freight forwarder typically files the cargo portion (called the "cargo control number" or CCN) on behalf of the shipper. This is the PARS (Pre-Arrival Review System) barcode on the bill of lading. The broker transmits this information to CBSA before the truck crosses.

**Conveyance data (the truck and driver):** The carrier is responsible for the conveyance (the truck itself) and crew (driver) data. For a single owner-operator, this means you file the conveyance and crew data yourself or through a service provider. For a driver working for a carrier, your dispatcher or the carrier's compliance department handles this — but you should confirm it has been done.

The practical implication: before crossing, you need to confirm both halves of the ACI filing are complete. The broker handles the cargo side. You (or your carrier) handle the conveyance side. If either side is missing, CBSA will not have a complete picture and the crossing will not go smoothly.

## Timing Requirements: The One-Hour Rule

**The CBSA requires ACI eManifest data to be submitted at least one hour before the carrier arrives at the border for highway crossings.**

This is not a soft guideline — it is a regulatory requirement. Filing at the border, in the commercial lane, is too late. CBSA officers need the advance data to do their job.

The practical implication: at your last US truck stop before the border, confirm your eManifest filing is complete and that CBSA has responded with an acceptance. Do not leave that stop without confirmation. A one-hour advance requirement means that if you are 45 minutes from the border when you check, you need to have filed before you left your last stop.

For Canadian destinations that are closer to the border — say, a delivery to Windsor or Fort Erie within an hour of the crossing — this means filing before you get on the highway for the final segment, not after.

## Documents You Need Before Filing

Before you or your carrier can complete the conveyance eManifest, you need:

**Carrier documents:**
- CBSA carrier code — a unique identifier issued by CBSA to registered carriers. US carriers crossing into Canada are required to obtain one. Your dispatcher or compliance department will have this.
- Your carrier's ACI eManifest Service Provider credentials if using a third-party software platform

**Driver/conveyance documents:**
- Commercial driver's licence number and issuing jurisdiction
- Passport or NEXUS card (recommended) or enhanced driver's licence number if applicable
- Truck licence plate number and registration jurisdiction
- Trailer licence plate number and registration jurisdiction
- USDOT number and MC number for the carrier

**Load documents:**
- PARS number (the cargo control number on your BOL that the broker filed)
- Bill of lading number
- Description of goods as declared in the cargo filing
- Country of origin of the goods
- Shipper and consignee details matching the cargo filing

**At the border**, the officer will want to see:
- Your commercial driver's licence
- The PARS barcode (printed on the BOL or a separate PARS sticker)
- Proof of ACI acceptance — either a CBSA response email/message or the acceptance number in your eManifest system

## How to File: The CBSA eManifest Portal

The CBSA operates a web-based eManifest portal accessible at emanifest.cbsa-asfc.gc.ca. For carriers who file infrequently, the portal is the direct filing option. For carriers who cross regularly, third-party ACI filing software integrates with CBSA's systems and automates much of the process.

**Portal-based filing process:**

1. **Log into the CBSA eManifest portal** with your carrier's registered credentials. First-time users need to register through the CBSA's My Business Account (formerly CBNC portal) using the carrier's business number.

2. **Create a new conveyance arrival.** Select "Highway" as the mode of transportation. Enter your carrier code, truck unit number, licence plate, jurisdiction, and driver information.

3. **Add crew data.** Enter the driver's name, date of birth, citizenship, and travel document information. For a solo driver, there is only one crew member.

4. **Attach the cargo.** Link your PARS number (the cargo control number your broker filed) to the conveyance record. This tells CBSA that the cargo filing and the conveyance filing are connected.

5. **Specify the crossing.** Select the port of entry (e.g., Windsor Ambassador Bridge, Peace Bridge at Fort Erie, Lacolle/Champlain). Entering an estimated crossing time helps CBSA officers manage lane queuing.

6. **Submit the manifest.** Upon submission, CBSA's system processes the data and responds. A "select" notice means CBSA wants the shipment for inspection — this does not necessarily mean a problem; it is part of their targeting process. A "release" or "no-query" response means the shipment is cleared for crossing without secondary inspection.

**Document the confirmation.** Save the CBSA response with the ACI reference number. You may need to produce this at the border or in the event of a compliance audit.

## Third-Party ACI Filing Services

For carriers or owner-operators who cross regularly, manual portal filing for every crossing is time-consuming. Third-party ACI filing services — software platforms or service bureaus — integrate with CBSA's system and handle conveyance filing as part of their dispatch or compliance workflow.

Several services specialize in cross-border ACI filing for US carriers:

- **OpenBorder** and **BorderConnect** are widely used ACI software platforms that integrate with dispatch systems and offer automated conveyance manifest generation.
- **1847 Financial** and other customs broker firms offer managed ACI service where their team files the conveyance manifest on your behalf for a per-crossing fee.
- **TMS platforms with ACI integration** — some Transportation Management Systems include ACI filing capability as part of their compliance module, so the filing is triggered automatically when a cross-border load is dispatched.

For owner-operators crossing occasionally (less than once per week), the CBSA portal is adequate. For frequent crossers, a third-party service pays for itself in time saved per crossing.

## Common Errors and How to Avoid Them

**Error: PARS not filed by broker before crossing.**
The cargo filing and the conveyance filing must both be submitted and accepted before crossing. If your broker has not filed the PARS, CBSA will not have cargo data — even if your conveyance manifest is perfect. Confirm the PARS status with your broker before departing the last US stop.

**Error: Carrier code not registered with CBSA.**
US carriers without a CBSA carrier code cannot file ACI eManifest correctly. If your carrier has not obtained a code, the application process takes 2–4 weeks. Do not attempt a Canada crossing without this code — you will be turned back at the border.

**Error: NEXUS/enhanced DL not current.**
If you use a NEXUS card or enhanced driver's licence for your travel document, verify it is not expired before crossing. A missed renewal is a surprisingly common reason for delays at the primary inspection plaza.

**Error: Manifest filed with incorrect trailer number.**
If you switch trailers after filing the manifest and before crossing, you must update the conveyance record with the new trailer information. Mismatched trailer information in the manifest versus the physical truck at the primary is a guaranteed secondary inspection.

**Error: Goods description does not match cargo filing.**
The conveyance manifest's goods description needs to align with what the broker filed in the cargo data. Generic descriptions ("freight all kinds") that do not match a specific cargo filing can trigger a hold.

## Penalties for Non-Compliance

CBSA has authority to issue administrative monetary penalties (AMPs) for ACI non-compliance. Penalties are assessed per shipment and can be significant:

- **Filing more than one hour late but before arrival:** first violation $1,000; repeat violations scale upward.
- **Filing at the border (no advance notice):** $2,000+ per violation.
- **Failure to file:** $8,000 per violation; carriers with multiple violations can face suspension of crossing privileges.

Beyond monetary penalties, non-compliant shipments result in mandatory secondary examination — adding time and cost to every crossing. Carriers with repeated ACI violations are flagged in CBSA's risk assessment system, which means more frequent secondary inspections on future crossings even when the filing is technically correct.

## Practical Checklist for Every Canada Crossing

At your last US stop (at least 60 minutes from the border):

- [ ] Confirm broker has filed PARS and received CBSA acceptance
- [ ] Confirm conveyance manifest submitted with correct truck, trailer, and driver data
- [ ] Save CBSA acceptance reference number
- [ ] Verify PARS barcode is printed and visible on bill of lading
- [ ] Confirm your commercial driver's licence, medical card, and travel document are current
- [ ] Check CBSA Border Wait Times portal for commercial lane estimated wait at your crossing

At the border:

- Present commercial driver's licence and travel document at primary
- Have printed PARS barcode available
- Be prepared to state commodity, shipper, and consignee if asked
- If directed to secondary, follow CBSA officer instructions and do not leave secondary without release authorization

Filing eManifest correctly is one of the most consistently mishandled compliance tasks for US drivers who cross occasionally. The one-hour advance requirement, the dual cargo/conveyance structure, and the CBSA portal interface are different enough from US border processes that drivers who assume the procedures are the same get it wrong. Following this checklist protects you from the costly delays and penalties that come from preventable non-compliance.
