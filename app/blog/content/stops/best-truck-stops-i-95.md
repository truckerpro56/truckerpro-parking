## I-95: America's Most Demanding Corridor for Truck Stop Planning

I-95 runs 1,920 miles from the Canada/Maine border at Houlton down to Miami, Florida. It is the longest north-south interstate on the East Coast and, mile for mile, one of the most difficult corridors to navigate for commercial drivers. The reasons are layered: dense urban zones through Boston, Providence, New York, Newark, Philadelphia, Baltimore, and Washington compress the spaces between viable truck stops into short windows. The Northeast parking deficit is severe — estimates suggest fewer available overnight spots per registered truck than nearly any other corridor in the country. And state-by-state regulatory variation means the rules governing weight, hours, and permitted routes shift repeatedly across a single long run.

Understanding which stops work on I-95, and why, is not a convenience — it is a prerequisite for running the corridor efficiently. This guide covers the top picks from north to south, with notes on parking capacity, amenities, and what to watch for at each.

## Maine and New Hampshire: Staging Northbound

I-95 enters the US from the Houlton, Maine port of entry with Canada. The corridor runs south through Bangor and Portland before crossing into New Hampshire at the Piscataqua River Bridge.

**Flying J at Bangor, ME (I-95 exit 180):** This is the primary full-service truck stop in northern Maine and the standard staging point for drivers running to or from New Brunswick. The Bangor Flying J has approximately 150 truck parking spaces, a sit-down restaurant, showers, and a CAT Scale. It fills up by 8:00 PM most weeknights — plan your arrival accordingly.

**Pilot Flying J at Wells, ME (I-95 exit 19):** The southern Maine hub, approximately 30 miles north of the New Hampshire border. Decent parking with around 120 spaces, fuel pricing that is competitive for the region. Useful for southbound drivers wanting to top off before entering Massachusetts pricing territory.

**Irving Oil at Kittery, ME (I-95 exit 3):** The last Maine stop before crossing into New Hampshire. Irving is the dominant Canadian-origin fuel brand in Maine and New England, and their Kittery location is a reliable fuel stop. Parking is more limited than the larger chain locations.

## Massachusetts to Connecticut: The Northeast Gauntlet

Massachusetts through Connecticut represents the most constrained segment of I-95. Truck stop spacing is compressed, parking is chronically scarce, and urban traffic makes timing critical.

**TA Truckstop at Milford, MA (I-95 exit 19):** One of the better-equipped stops in this dense corridor. The Milford TA has a sit-down restaurant, full shower facilities, a well-stocked driver store, and approximately 100 truck parking spaces — better than most options in eastern Massachusetts. It is heavily used; arrive before 5:00 PM or expect competition for spots.

**Pilot Flying J at Plainfield, CT (I-395 junction area):** For drivers using I-395 to bridge I-95 and I-84, the Plainfield Pilot is a practical option with good amenities. On I-95 proper in Connecticut, stops are sparse and parking is constrained.

**Love's Travel Stop at East Windsor, CT:** Off I-91 near Hartford, this is a useful stop for drivers who route inland to avoid I-95 congestion in Connecticut — a tactic many experienced Northeast hands employ.

## New York and New Jersey: Maximum Difficulty Parking

The I-95 stretch through New York and New Jersey is genuinely difficult. Legal truck routes, height restrictions on major bridges and tunnels, and an almost complete absence of practical overnight parking near the urban core create conditions where planning is mandatory, not optional.

**Travel Centers of America at Avenel, NJ (Exit 12 area):** One of the few full-service stops in the New Jersey corridor. The Avenel TA is a large facility by Northeast standards — approximately 200 truck spaces, a restaurant, showers, and a truck service bay. It fills to capacity by 6:00 PM on most weeknights. If you need to overnight in the Metro area, the Avenel TA and the nearby Petro at Carteret are the practical choices.

**Petro Stopping Center at Carneys Point, NJ (I-295 at exit 1):** Near the Delaware Memorial Bridge, this Petro location is the effective anchor stop for drivers transiting the Delaware River crossing. Over 300 parking spaces, a full-service restaurant, Petro's repair shop, and CAT scale. It is south enough to be slightly less pressured than the urban core stops.

**Flying J at South Hackensack, NJ:** A useful fuel stop for drivers navigating the northern New Jersey approach, but parking is limited. Treat it as a fuel stop, not an overnight option.

## Delaware, Maryland, and Virginia: The Mid-Atlantic Sweet Spot

Once clear of the New York/New Jersey urban zone, I-95 opens up and truck stop quality improves significantly.

**Love's Travel Stop at Newark, DE (I-95 at exit 1B):** Just south of the Delaware Memorial Bridge, the Newark Love's is a modern, well-maintained facility. Good parking, reliable shower availability, Speedco on site, and competitive fuel pricing made better by Delaware's lower fuel taxes. A strong choice for the northern Delaware stop.

**Pilot Flying J at Aberdeen, MD (I-95 exit 85):** The primary north-of-Baltimore staging stop. The Aberdeen Pilot has around 200 truck spaces, good amenities, and is conveniently positioned for drivers who want to clear Baltimore during off-peak hours. Running Baltimore between 7:00 PM and 5:00 AM avoids the worst traffic on I-95 through the harbor tunnel.

**TA Truckstop at Jessup, MD (I-95 exit 41):** Between Baltimore and Washington, the Jessup TA is the best-positioned full-service stop in this segment. Approximately 150 spaces, full restaurant, and a repair shop. If you time a break here to clear the Washington beltway during off-peak hours, the fuel stop pays back in time.

**Pilot Flying J at Woodbridge, VA (I-95 exit 158B):** South of the Washington beltway, the Woodbridge Pilot is heavily used and often tight on parking. Arrive early or fuel and continue south.

## North Carolina and South Carolina: The Deep South Transition

I-95 through the Carolinas transitions from the constrained Northeast to the more open Southeast. Stop quality and parking availability both improve markedly.

**Petro Stopping Center at Selma, NC (I-95 exit 97):** One of the best full-service stops on the entire I-95 corridor by driver reviews. The Selma Petro has over 250 truck parking spaces, a full-service Country Pride restaurant, well-maintained showers, and Petro's certified repair shop. Consistently well-rated for cleanliness and service quality. If you are running I-95 northbound and can stage here, do it.

**Love's Travel Stop at Santee, SC (I-95 exit 98):** A newer Love's facility in the mid-South Carolina corridor. Clean, modern, with good parking and Speedco service. The Santee exit cluster also has competitive fuel pricing driven by multiple options at the interchange.

**Pilot Flying J at Walterboro, SC (I-95 exit 57):** Another solid option in the southern Carolina corridor, with good amenities and reliable overnight parking.

## Georgia and Florida: Running to the Sun

**Flying J at Kingsland, GA (I-95 exit 3):** Near the Florida state line, the Kingsland Flying J is the standard last-stop-before-Florida staging point. Good parking, full amenities, and competitive Georgia fuel pricing before you enter Florida's slightly higher fuel tax structure.

**Love's Travel Stop at Jacksonville, FL (multiple locations along I-95/I-295):** The Jacksonville metro area has several Love's and Pilot locations on or near I-95. The Love's at Baldwin, FL (near I-10/I-95 interchange) is particularly well-positioned for north-south corridor drivers.

**Pilot Flying J at Ocala, FL (I-75/I-95 connects via US 301 area):** For drivers running I-95 to I-75 transitions in Florida, Ocala is the practical hub. Multiple chains compete here, driving competitive fuel pricing.

**TA Truckstop at Fort Pierce, FL (I-95 exit 129):** A full-service stop in the mid-Florida corridor, well-equipped and reliably maintained. Good staging point for drivers making South Florida deliveries.

## I-95 Planning: Key Principles

**Book parking before you need it.** I-95 from Maryland to Connecticut has the worst parking-to-truck ratio of any major corridor. Use Pilot's app, Love's app, or TruckPark to check availability as you approach your planned stop time.

**Watch state fuel tax transitions.** New York and Connecticut have notably higher diesel taxes than Virginia, Delaware, and the Carolinas. Fuel in Virginia before entering DC/Maryland, and in Maine before entering Massachusetts.

**Avoid peak traffic windows in the urban zones.** Boston (7–9 AM, 4–7 PM), New York/NJ (6–9 AM, 3–8 PM), and Washington DC (7–9 AM, 4–7 PM) add hours to a run. Staging stops are not just for rest — they are active traffic avoidance tools on I-95.

**Use the inland alternative.** For drivers who can choose their routing, I-81 through Virginia and Pennsylvania is less congested, has better truck stop spacing, and often faster point-to-point times than I-95 through the Northeast corridor.
