## Why Booking in Advance Beats Hunting on Arrival

The days of pulling into a truck stop and expecting a spot to be available are fading fast. Canada's parking shortage — estimated at 10,000 spaces nationwide by the Canadian Trucking Alliance — means that in the GTA corridor, Lower Mainland BC, and the Montreal-Quebec City run, lot capacity is gone by 8 or 9 PM on most weekday nights. Drivers who don't plan ahead spend 45 minutes or more circling, burning fuel, eating into their off-duty time, and increasing stress levels at the end of an already long shift.

Online booking solves this. A reservation made before you leave the shipper guarantees your spot is waiting when you arrive. The total time investment once you know the system: under two minutes per booking. This guide walks you through the process step by step and covers the tools available to Canadian truckers right now.

## What to Look for in a Parking Platform

Not all online parking platforms are built for commercial trucks. Consumer parking apps like SpotHero or ParkWhiz are designed for passenger cars and have no concept of a 53-foot trailer or a B-train combination. Before you invest time in any platform, confirm it meets these minimum criteria.

**Commercial vehicle support.** The platform should allow you to specify your vehicle configuration — tractor-trailer, super B, tandem, single axle — and filter results to locations physically capable of accommodating your unit. A parking map that shows you a suburban parkade is worse than useless.

**Verified location data.** Look for platforms that physically verify their listed locations or at minimum have recent driver reviews. A listing that hasn't been reviewed in two years may no longer exist, may have changed ownership, or may have reduced its security infrastructure.

**Clear amenity information.** You should be able to see, before booking, whether the facility has overnight lighting, perimeter fencing, showers, shore power hookups, fuel, and a driver's lounge. These details affect both your safety and your comfort.

**Cancellation and refund policy.** Routes change, loads get re-routed, and hours-of-service reality doesn't always match dispatch planning. Choose a platform with a reasonable cancellation window — ideally free cancellation up to 2 hours before the booking window opens.

**Mobile-accessible confirmation.** Your booking confirmation should be available on your phone as a QR code, reference number, or gate code that a lot attendant can verify without requiring you to print anything or log in to a website on a laptop.

**Step 2: Filter by your requirements.**
Apply filters for the amenities you need: secured fencing, overnight lighting, shore power, showers. If you're driving a combination longer than 53 feet, filter for facilities that explicitly accommodate longer vehicles. Narrow your results to locations with availability on your target date.

**Step 3: Read the location profile.**
Before booking, open the location detail page. Review the security features, surface type (paved vs. gravel), operating hours, access instructions, and recent driver reviews. **Step 4: Select your dates and vehicle configuration.**
Choose your arrival date and estimated check-in time. Enter your vehicle configuration. The platform calculates availability and confirms the nightly rate including applicable provincial taxes (GST, HST, or QST depending on the province).

Provide your payment information — Visa, Mastercard, or fleet card. Your confirmation appears immediately in-app and is sent to your email. The confirmation includes the facility address, any gate or access codes, check-in instructions, and the operator's phone number for after-hours issues.

Total time from search to confirmed booking: 90 seconds to 2 minutes once you're familiar with the interface.

## Mobile Booking: Tips for On-the-Road Use

The majority of truck parking bookings are made on smartphones, either from a cab or from a driver lounge. A few practices make mobile booking smoother.

**Save your vehicle profile.** Most platforms allow you to store your vehicle configuration — unit length, trailer type, combination class — so you don't re-enter it with every booking. Set this up the first time you use the platform and all subsequent searches will automatically filter for compatible facilities.

**Use location services.** Allowing the app to access your GPS location means you can search "near me" rather than typing an address. When you're 45 minutes from your planned overnight stop and want to confirm your reservation or book on the fly, location-based search is faster.

**Enable booking notifications.** Turn on push notifications from your parking app. Notifications will alert you when a facility confirms your booking, when your check-in window is approaching, and if there are any issues with access on arrival. Some platforms also send alerts when facilities you haven't booked at reach capacity, which is useful context for planning.

**Pre-fill your payment method.** Don't wait until you're booking at 7 PM with the sun setting to set up your payment details. Add your credit card or fleet card to the platform at the terminal before you leave, so the payment step at booking time is one tap rather than a manual entry.

**Screenshot your confirmation.** If your route passes through areas with poor cell signal — northern Ontario, the Coquihalla, the Trans-Canada through New Brunswick — screenshot the confirmation before you lose connectivity. Gate codes and access instructions on a screenshot work without a data connection.

## Payment Tips: Fleet Cards, Personal Cards, and Receipts

Parking is a legitimate, reimbursable expense for employed drivers and a tax-deductible operating expense for owner-operators. Managing the payment trail properly saves time and money.

**Use your fleet card where accepted.** Many Canadian parking operators now accept major fleet cards (WEX, Comdata, FleetCor, Milepoint). Paying with a fleet card automatically captures the expense against your operator account and generates a receipt for your fleet manager, eliminating the need to keep paper receipts.

**Request itemized receipts.** For tax purposes, your receipt should show the facility name and address, the date, the vehicle type, and the nightly rate. A generic credit card receipt showing a dollar amount is not sufficient for CRA expense documentation. Most platforms generate PDF receipts that include all required fields.

**For owner-operators: track parking separately.** CRA allows owner-operators to deduct commercial parking expenses as a business operating cost. Keep a log of dates, locations, and amounts. The digital records from a booking platform are an excellent starting point — download a monthly transaction summary and file it with your other expense records.

## What to Do If Your Spot Isn't Available on Arrival

Even with a confirmed booking, occasionally operational problems occur at facilities — a broken gate, an overbooked lot, or a facility that has closed unexpectedly. Here is how to handle it.

First, call the facility operator using the number in your confirmation immediately on arrival. Do not spend time waiting — call right away and describe the problem. In most cases, a direct conversation with the operator resolves access issues within a few minutes.

If the operator cannot resolve the issue, contact the booking platform's support line. Most Canadian platforms have 24/7 support for exactly this situation. They can either arrange alternative access or identify a nearby alternative facility at no additional cost.

Document the problem before you leave — take a photo of the inaccessible gate or the full lot, and note the time. This documentation supports a refund request and helps the platform improve listing accuracy.

Always have a backup facility identified before you make your primary booking. On the same search session where you booked your primary spot, note one alternative 20–30 km in either direction. If your primary location has a problem, you already know your backup and can navigate directly without searching under pressure.

## Building Booking Into Your Route Planning Routine

The most effective truck drivers treat parking booking the same way they treat fuel planning — it happens before the truck moves, not at the end of the day. Build the habit of booking your overnight stop as part of your pre-trip preparation: load confirmed, delivery window confirmed, border paperwork filed, parking booked.

For multi-day runs, book all your overnight stops at once. Booking two or three nights simultaneously takes less total time than booking each night individually, and it means you have no decisions to make at the end of a tiring day — just navigate to the confirmed address and check in.

The two minutes you spend booking a spot before you leave your shipper are an investment that pays back in eliminated search time, lower stress, better rest quality, and a stronger compliance record. In high-demand corridors, they may also be the difference between a legal, secure overnight and an improvised situation that creates risk for your cargo, your licence, and your health.
